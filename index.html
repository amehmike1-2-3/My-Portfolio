5 Things Every Small Business Must Post on Social Media

By Neyo Digital April 2025 5 min read

Most small business owners make the same mistake — they either don’t post at all or post randomly with no strategy. Then they wonder why nobody is buying. The truth is, social media is the most powerful free marketing tool available — but only if you use it correctly.

In this article, I’ll show you the exact 5 types of posts that every business should be making consistently to attract attention, build trust and convert followers into paying customers.

💡 Key Insight
“Consistency beats perfection. One good post every day beats one perfect post every month.”

The 5 Post Types You Need

01
The Quote Post

Inspirational or business quotes build your brand personality and get shared massively. People love saving and sharing quotes that resonate with them. Use quotes related to your niche — business, money, hustle, success. This keeps your page active even when you have nothing to sell that day.

02
The Promo Post

This is your direct sales post. Show your product, state the price clearly, and tell people exactly how to buy it. Don’t be shy about this — people need to be told what to do. Use urgency like “Limited Time” or “Only 5 Left” to push people to act fast. Post this 2–3 times per week.

03
The Tip Post

Share free valuable tips related to your niche. If you sell budget planners, share money saving tips. If you sell social media templates, share posting strategies. When you give value for free, people trust you — and trust leads to sales. This is called authority building and it’s one of the most powerful things you can do.

04
The Testimonial Post

Social proof is everything. When someone sees that real people have bought from you and are happy, their resistance to buying drops immediately. Screenshot any good feedback you receive and turn it into a post. Even if you’re new and have no reviews yet — ask your first customers for feedback and post it immediately.

05
The Announcement Post

New product dropping? Price change? Bundle deal? Tell your followers first. Announcements create excitement and urgency. They make your followers feel like insiders — like they’re part of something. Use phrases like “New Drop”, “Just Launched”, “Limited Offer” to drive engagement and clicks.

How Often Should You Post?

The minimum for any business serious about growth is once per day. Rotate through the 5 post types above and you’ll never run out of content ideas.

📅 Weekly Schedule
Mon: Quote · Tue: Promo · Wed: Tip · Thu: Testimonial · Fri: Promo · Sat: Announcement · Sun: Quote

How to Set Your Freelance Rates Without Losing Clients

By Neyo Digital March 2025 4 min read

One of the biggest mistakes Nigerian freelancers make is undercharging. They think lowering their price will attract more clients. In reality, it does the opposite — it makes you look cheap and untrustworthy.

💡 Truth
“Clients don’t just pay for your skill — they pay for your confidence and positioning.”

How to Price Yourself Correctly

01
Know Your Minimum

Calculate your monthly expenses and divide by the number of clients you can handle. That’s your floor. Never go below it.

02
Research the Market Rate

Check what other Nigerian freelancers charge for the same service on Fiverr, Upwork and local groups. Position yourself in the mid-to-high range.

03
Use a Professional Rate Card

Sending a well-designed rate card instead of a WhatsApp message instantly positions you as a professional. Clients take you more seriously and rarely negotiate down.

Most Nigerian entrepreneurs earn decent money but still feel broke at the end of the month. The problem is never the income — it’s the lack of a system to manage it.

What a Monthly Budget Does For You

Stops Impulsive Spending

When you have a written plan, you think twice before every purchase. That alone saves most people ₦20,000+ monthly.

02
Shows You Where Your Money Goes

Most people are shocked when they track their spending for the first time. Data gives you power to change habits.

03
Helps You Save & Invest

When you budget first, savings become automatic. You stop saving what’s left — you spend what’s left after saving.
